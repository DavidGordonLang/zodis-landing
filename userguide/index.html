What Å½odis is, and what it is not

Å½odis is not a course.

It does not teach Lithuanian in a fixed order.

It does not expect you to memorise lists before you are allowed to speak.

Å½odis is a personal language library.

You collect words and phrases that matter to you.

Single words matter just as much as full phrases.

You add them when they matter, when you need them, think of them, or are simply curious.

This mirrors real life. You rarely need lesson seven grammar. You need this word, right now.

The core idea

You learn faster when language connects to real situations.

Å½odis is built around three principles:

  • You learn what you need, when you need it
  • Meaning comes from context, not repetition
  • You stay in control of your data and your progress

Everything in the app supports these ideas.

Adding words and phrases

You can add entries in two ways:

  • From the Home screen using translation
  • Directly in the Library using Add Entry

Each entry can include:

  • English
  • Lithuanian
  • Phonetic spelling
  • Category
  • Notes
  • RAG status

You are not required to fill everything in. Start simple. You can always expand later.

Translator input flexibility

Å½odis is flexible about how you enter language.

You can enter English to receive a Lithuanian translation, but you can also enter Lithuanian directly. The app will still process the phrase correctly and store it in your library.

This is useful if you already know some Lithuanian and want to check meaning, phrasing, or form. It is also useful if you hear something spoken and want to capture it exactly as it was said.

You can even hand the app to a Lithuanian speaker and ask them to type the phrase directly. Å½odis will store it cleanly, ready for audio playback, notes, and organisation.

This makes Å½odis more than a one way translator. It becomes a tool for collecting authentic language from real people and real situations.

Audio playback

Audio is central to how Å½odis works.

  • Tap a phrase to hear it at normal speed
  • Long press to hear it slowly

This lets you hear natural pronunciation first, then slow it down when needed.

Use audio often. Even passive listening builds familiarity.

Notes are where learning deepens

The Notes field is one of the most important parts of Å½odis.

Use Notes to capture things like:

  • When a phrase sounds natural and when it does not
  • Alternative forms or word order
  • Cultural context
  • Why a translation is the way it is

Over time, your Notes become a personalised explanation layer that no generic course can give you.
